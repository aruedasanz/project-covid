Beyond Case-Counts: Excess deaths provide a deeper understanding of COVID-19 in America

This website goes beyond basic case counts to understand the behavior of COVID-19 across different states in the US. We believe that case counts and official COVID-19 deaths are useful to identify outbreaks. However, these figures fail to identify how lethal the virus is with respect to other diseases because COVID-19 case and death tolls:

  • Don't account for non-tested individuals
  • Don't account for non-covid preventable deaths at overloaded hospitals

These figures confound the effectiveness of policies across different geographies. For this reason, epidemiologists analyze Excess Mortality: how much more deaths per month occured during the pandemic, compared to an average year for each state. For more information, you can click here.

Below, we provide an overview of excess death figures by state. We also show how this figure is more effective than COVID-19 cases and death tolls at explaining the health effects of the pandemic.


Excess Deaths by State

Estimates of excess deaths provide information about the burden of the COVID-19 pandemic, including deaths that are directly or indirectly attributed to COVID-19.

  • Excess deaths from COVID-19 are:the difference between the observed number of deaths on a week during the pandemic and before the pandemic.

In the map, we are calculating a weekly average for each state. This average doesn't account for different circumstances in each state. For instance, while the average excess mortality in New York is similar to North Dakota, it is worth noting the peak occurred at different months. In New York, the peak in excess deaths occurred in the first months of the pandemic, while in North Dakota, this happened later. These differences occurred because while New York is more connected to international travel, it struck earlier than in North Dakota.
